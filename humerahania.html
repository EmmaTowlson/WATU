What Antarctica Taught Me

"12 months Homeward Bound journey is transformational but the backdrop of Antarctica is transitional phase for me in developing my leadership skills, decision making and collective actions for saving the planet earth. I dive deeply to unlock my potential when I was on ship surrounded with amazing women in STEMM (science, technology, engineering, mathematics, medicine) leaders, beauty of the frozen continent and diversity/challenges of wild life. The four core development components (leadership, strategy, visibility and science) of the program has significantly improved my leadership skills (clarity in actions and more confidence), broadened my vision and now my perspective of thinking/actions is more strategic and proactive. I learned that leadership is a long term race when women collaborate for the sustainable future. Climate change is a global threat and meaningful global collaboration help women to justify their role for taking authentic actions for saving next generation. I found the visibility stream very interesting to set the personal and professional goals. After coming back I tried to involve my team to understand their values and provided my back to take courage as I learned courage is contagious. With every passing day I am transferring the tools which are helpful for the team like BRAVING trust frame work (boundary, reliability, accountability, integrity, vault, non-judgmental and generosity) by Brene Brown (social scantiest). I involved farm advisors (male and female) from various organisations (research/academia, commercial, not for profit organisations and government) in the discussion of diversity and inclusion with their organisation that how unconscious stereotypic thinking linked with social preferences and behaviors. I shared that how we are mainstreaming gender at our workplace and in the field a-ha moment when I take a breath in Antarctica and see the beauty around."

Humera

Iqbal
